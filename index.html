About Me

Hi! I'm Yiming, a second-year PhD student in National University of Singapore, very fortunate to be advised by Prof. Dianbo Liu. I have broad research interests in generative models, vision-language models, mechanistic interpretability, and AI for medicine. My research aims to develop algorithms and theories toward a better understanding of intelligence by identifying human-interpretable concepts encoded in both machine learning models and the human brain. During my undergraduate studies, I was lucky to work with Prof. Bin Dong and Prof. Shanghang Zhang.

Research Interests

My research interests focus on:

  • Mechanistic Interpretability: Understanding the internal representation space of machine learning models by identifying specific concepts encoded within them.
  • Neuroscience: Understanding internal representations in the human brain through EEG and fMRI signals, identifying meaningful patterns in neural activity.
  • Medical AI Transparency: Developing interpretable diagnostic systems that enhance clinical trust and improve healthcare outcomes.

Beyond these specific research directions, I'm also broadly interested in vision-language models, AI safety, generalization, and automatic AI agents.
